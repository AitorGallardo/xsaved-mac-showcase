# Product

## The problem

The desktop is where you sit down to actually use what you've saved — to search it, sort it, and pull things back out. X gives you the same flat, unsearchable list there as everywhere else. And underneath that is a quieter problem: there's no good way to keep a clean, current copy of your bookmarks at all.

## The idea

A desktop app that solves both: a genuinely nice place to browse and search your library, and the engine that keeps that library current and shared with your other devices — without anyone having to think about it.

## What it does

- A desktop library: an adaptive grid, full-text search, a command palette, sidebar filters, a detail inspector, and drag-and-drop into other apps.
- Background sync that keeps the library current and quietly updates your other devices.
- A local copy of your bookmarks that stays available and survives the original tweet being deleted.

## Principles

**Feel native.** The bar is an app that looks at home next to the system's own — native window chrome, pointer-aware throughout, restrained and calm.

**Do the heavy work here.** The desktop is the right place for the demanding parts, so the phone can stay a light, fast reader.

**Quiet by default.** It keeps things current in the background and stays out of the way until you open it.

**One family.** It shares its design language and its backend with the other XSaved apps, so they feel like one product across platforms.
